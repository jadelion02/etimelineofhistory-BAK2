歷史時間表記錄包含三段時期：1.創造-遠離的過去(從創造天地到創造亞當和夏娃), 2.歷史-近期的過去(從創造亞當和夏娃到現在), 及 3.啟示-未來(從現在到新天新地)：

近期的過去歷史時間表包括聖經中的著名人物和事件從亞當到使徒保羅, 以及從三皇五帝到蔡英文, 習近平, 以及從夏朝到中華民國, 中華人民共和國, 和從大禹治水到香港及澳門回歸中國。

要用應用程序，請打開瀏覽器((Browser), 複製並粘貼下面的鏈接並將其放在 URL 空間中, 然後按 Enter 鍵。鏈接到歷史的 eTimeline of History https://jadelion02.github.io/etimelineofhistory.

歷史時間表基於希伯來日曆, 從"1"開始(即"零"年, 1 是相當於希伯來拉比傳統的公元前 3761年), 但是當您將鼠標光標懸停在感興趣的人物和事件點上時, 它便會顯示公曆中的年份。歷史時間表的使用方法 是列在標題旁邊的小綠色方塊下面的問號(即是？). 列出。只需將鼠標光標懸停在它上面, 就會顯示一個下拉用法列表。如果你有一個觸摸屏，你可以用手指控制它們以及屏幕的壓縮和擴展(即相當於屏幕頂部的 + 和 - 標誌)。

在屏幕控制頂部, 您會看到一個白色矩形框, 框中帶有"1"(即是 ), 這是應用程序的默認搜索值。如果您知道或記得人物角色或事件的 ID 號, 請在框中鍵入數字, 然後按放大鏡“搜索”按鈕(即是 ) 它便會跳轉到該時間點。例 如, "1.01"將帶您到諾亞, "1.02"將帶您到亞伯拉罕。沙漏旁邊的是複選框是用於在"活動/非活動""搜索"按鈕之間切換。在此復選框的左側是一 個較寬大的白色下拉框, 在框的右側有一個向下箭頭 (即是 ). 使用鼠標光標單按此框會顯示的下拉列表"菜單"。下拉列表包含大多數值得注意的人物和事件, 這些人物和事件將允許 您進行選擇, 然後在按下"搜索"按鈕時便跳轉到該人物和事件。但是, 在觸摸屏上使用手指時, 人物和事件之間的間距會更開。

歷史時間表該應用在顯示器上閱讀, 但尚未方便在手機屏幕或其他小型移動設備上閱讀。但是，您仍然可以 使用平板電腦或手機連接到應用程序, 但屏幕的較大部分將被標題佔用。

使用以下瀏覽器可以作最佳地查看歷史時間表：Chrome, Opera, Internet Explorer, MS Edge 或 iOS Safari; 其他瀏覽器(如 Firefox)可能無法正常運行。

Biblical, Chinese & World History Timeline

eTimeline of History consists of three timeline periods: 1. Creation - the distance past (from the creation of the heaven and the earth to the creation of Adam and Eve), 2. History - the immediate past (from the creation of Adam and Eve to present), and 3. Revelation - the future (from present to new heaven and new earth):

The immediate past history includes notable characters and events of the Bible from Adam to Apostle Paul, and of the Chinese history from 3 Sovereigns & 5 Emperors to Tsai Ing-wen, Xi Jinping, and dynasties from Xia Dynasty to Republic of China (中華民國), People Republic of China (中华人民共和国), and events from taming of the great flood by Yu the Great (大禹治水) to the return of Hongkong and Macau to China (香港及澳門回歸中國).

To access the app, open a browser, copy and paste the link below and place it in the URL space and press the Enter key. Link to eTimeline of History https://jadelion02.github.io/etimelineofhistory.

The timeline is based on Hebrew Calendar starting at “1" (i.e. zero year, 1 is equivalent to 3,761 BC based on Hebrew Rabbinical tradition), however when you hover the mouse cursor over the point of interest, the years in Gregorian Calendar is shown in the pop-up tool tip. The explanations of the commands are listed under the little green square next to the title by a question mark (i.e. ). Just hover the mouse cursor over it and a drop down menu will be shown. If you have a touch screen, you can use your finger to control them as well as the compression and expansion of the screen (i.e. which is equivalent to the + and – signs on top of the screen).

At the top of the control screen, you will see a white rectangular box with a "1” in the box (i.e. ), this is the default search value of the app. If you know or remember the ID number of the character or event, type the number in the box and press the magnifying glass or the Search button (i.e. ) to jump to that point in time. For example, 1.01 will take you to Noah, and 1.02 will take you to Abraham. The check box next to the hour glass is to toggle between Activate/Inactive the Search button. To the left of this check box is a wide white drop-down box with a down arrow on the right-hand side of the box (i.e. ). Clicking this box with the mouse cursor will cause a drop-down to appear. The drop-down list includes most of the notable characters which will allow you to make your selection and then jump to that characters when the Search button is pressed. However, the spacing in between the characters and events is farther apart when using your finger on a touch screen.

The app is written to be read on a monitor, but not on a cell phone screen or other small mobile devices as yet. However you could still use a tablet or cell phone to connect to the app, but the larger part of the screen will be taken up by the header.

The timeline is best viewed using the following browser: Chrome Browser, Opera Browser, Internet Explorer, MS Edge and iOS Safari; other browser such as Firefox may not work properly.
